The Process of Conducting Research: An Overview

Step 1: Identify and Create a Topic.

First and foremost, there is always a matter, issue, or subject analyzed and examined in a research paper. For starters, you must always begin by determining the subject. Select one that best interests you and one you especially want to talk about.

Step 2: Look for Information.

After identifying your chosen topic, try to check if you have enough resources available. If that's the case, you may continue and start by specifying which materials to use for your research. Otherwise, it would be much more difficult to continue with little information. As the internet is very prevalent nowadays, search engines are more advanced. So, by using search engines, such as Google, Firefox, etc. it would be easier to locate the references required. We have a recommended list of websites down below for you to check out! Here is also a video that may help you choose which type of source you want to use.

Step 3: Analyze the Given Information.

Once you have located the resources you're going to use, assess if it has credible and reliable data. Evidence and facts are necessary as they are utilized in every research paper, so it is an important detail to take note of. The attached video helps as a guide for finding out whether or not the information is conceivable.

Step 4: Organize the Information and Write the Paper.

Begin by writing a draft or outline to help organize your ideas, revise and edit your draft, and ultimately, create the final product. The succeeding photos presented are examples of an outline.

Step 5: Cite Sources and Proofread.

And finally! The last step of the process is citing your sources properly and proofreading the paper you have created. Ensure that there are no spelling and grammar mistakes, and cite your references appropriately. The APA and MLA formats are the citations widely employed, and this video will help guide you on how to use them.

Recommended Websites for Research

More Online Articles as a Guide for Conducting Research

Google Scholar

Google Scholar provides a simple way to broadly search for scholarly literature. From one place, you can search across many disciplines and sources: articles, theses, books, abstracts and court opinions, from academic publishers, professional societies, online repositories, universities and other web sites.

PubMed

PubMed is a free resource supporting the search and retrieval of biomedical and life sciences literature with the aim of improving healthâ€“both globally and personally. The PubMed database contains more than 34 million citations and abstracts of biomedical literature.

ResearchGate

ResearchGate is a European commercial social networking site for scientists and researchers to share papers, ask and answer questions, and find collaborators.

JSTOR

JSTOR is a digital library of academic content in many formats and disciplines. The collections include top peer-reviewed scholarly journals as well as respected literary journals, academic monographs, research reports from trusted institutes, and diverse primary sources.
