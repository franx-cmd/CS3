Test -/20

1. My watch is set correctly at 9 A.M. If it loses 2 minutes every hour, what will be the time on my watch if the correct time is 4 P.M. the next day?

2. By what number should √1/4 be multiplied to give the result 1?

3. James answered a 40-item examination. He gets 5 points for every correct answer but he loses 2 points for every wrong answer. What was his total score if he correctly answered 75% of the items?

4. Thirty-six men can finish building a house in 120 days. If a third of the original number of men were added, how many days would take the whole group to finish the same house?

5. What is the next term of the sequence: 2, 5, 11, 23, …?

6. On his first birthday, Rico placed on P5 coin in a jar. On his second birthday, he placed two P5 coins in the same jar. Every birthday he had thereafter, he put in as many P5 coins as his age in years. If he now has a total of P140 in the jar, how old is he?

7. What is the missing number in the circle?


8. The faces of a big cube were painted. Then it was divided into 27 smaller cubes of the same size. How many small cubes have only one face painted?

9. Eight unit cubes are glued to form a 2 by 2 by 2 cube. If one corner unit cube is removed, what is the total surface area of the resulting figure?

10. The perimeter of any given polygon can be solved by getting the sum of all its sides. If the perimeter of the polygon given on the right is 72 inches (in), and the length of the sides A and B are equal, what is the length of side A?


11. Two identical regular hexagons (6-sided polygons with equal length of sides) were placed on top of each other as shown on the figure below. If the perimeter (total length of the sides) of one hexagon is 30 inches, what is the total perimeter of the resulting figure?


12. An ant walks around the edges of different shapes? In which shape will the ant walk the SHORTEST distance?

13. The figure drawn is rectangle ABCD. What is the value of a?


14. A rectangle has an area of 100 cm^2. The lengths of its sides are doubled to form a new rectangle. How does the area of the new rectangle compare with the area of the original rectangle?

15. Carol has a rectangular sheet of paper measuring 48 cm by 72 cm paper. She wants to cut it into the LEAST number of square pieces without wasting any material. How many squares can she cut from the paper?

For the last five questions, ONLY type in a number.

16. A teacher gave a three-item TRUE or FALSE quiz. How many possible combinations of answers can students have?

17. What is the sum of the greatest 3-digit number and the greatest 4-digit number?

18. I need 15 pieces of similar fruits to make 3 glasses of juice. How many pieces of the same fruit do I need to make a dozen glasses of juice?

19. How many fifty-peso bills are equivalent to 30 twenty-peso bills?

20. 62.5% of 280 is what percent of 200?
